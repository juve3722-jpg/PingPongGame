🍪 Browser Storage Notice

Cookies Policy

Last Updated: June 11, 2026

This document explains what cookies are, how Spin the Wheel Game (https://spinwheelgame.github.io/spin-wheel-game) utilizes them, and how your web browser manages localized data properties to enhance your random spinner experience[cite: 1].

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are simple text fragments loaded onto your computer or smartphone by web servers when opening specific URLs. They allow websites to remember your unique sessions, remember your general configurations, and save your visual themes.

2. Our Use of Local Storage and Session Values

Unlike traditional servers that rely heavily on databases, our application uses modern web sandbox features called Local Storage and Session Storage[cite: 2]. This functions exactly like cookies but features better efficiency and higher security margins:

  • Saving Presets & Labels: When you modify your list of items (such as custom choice configurations or food lists), those strings are safely written right to your browser's Local Storage[cite: 1, 2]. This ensures that when you refresh the window tomorrow, your custom setup isn't wiped clean![cite: 1]
  • UI Preferences: Chosen color palettes (Classic, Pastel, Neon, etc.), toggle choices for turning satisfying sound systems on/off, and specific spin speeds are kept saved on your device[cite: 1, 2].

Crucially, this operational data is kept in your device sandbox—meaning it is never transmitted to an external server[cite: 2].

3. Third-Party Tracker Cookies

We may deploy third-party system components to study global traffic volumes and support our operational costs through advertisements. These services (such as Google Analytics) drop analytics tracking cookies onto your device to report anonymized data metrics, like regional visitor numbers, device classes, and user navigation loops.

4. Your Configuration Controls

You can instruct your web browser to delete cookies, refuse automatic cookie acceptance, or erase current Local Storage setups via your browser settings panel. Be aware that doing so will clear your custom text entries, history logs, and palette modifications, resetting the app to default values[cite: 1, 2].
